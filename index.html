Hey Ghost,
I'm Mariana👋

I know you're not hiring — and I wasn’t exactly invited to send a cover letter either. But here I am anyway, making my case.

I could’ve just sent a PDF. But that didn’t feel very Ghost. So I made this instead — something simple, structured, and (hopefully) useful. My attempt at speaking your language.

This page is a reflection of how I work: with curiosity, care, and a clear purpose. No templates, no web experience — just a real desire to connect with a team I truly admire.

This all started with a question I wrote about in my blog: “What drives you?” In that post, I reflected on my journey to find the kind of work — and the kind of team — I want to be part of. That reflection is what brings me here.

1. So, what drives me?

Writing that blog post helped me get clarity: I want to spend my time doing work I enjoy, for a product I genuinely care about, alongside people who work in a way that aligns with my values.

So, what kind of work gets me excited? In short: helping teams work better. Simplifying what’s messy. Organizing what’s scattered. Supporting operations so everything (and everyone) runs smoother — not by adding more, but by removing friction and creating structure where it’s needed most.

I like making things click. Whether that means cleaning up a process, redesigning a workflow, or documenting something so well no one ever needs to ask about it again. That’s the kind of work I want to keep doing, ideally in a place where both the product and the culture feel like a natural fit.

2.And, why you?

I’ve admired Ghost for a long time — not just for the product, but for the way you work.

You’ve built something powerful without the usual noise. A tool that helps people communicate clearly and purposefully, in a world that’s overflowing with content. That kind of focus really stands out.

But what I admire most is the culture behind it: simplicity, independence, transparency. A small, thoughtful team doing meaningful work, without chasing trends or playing corporate games. That’s the kind of place I’d love to be part of.

It’s not just that I like what you make — I like how you think. And that’s why I wanted to reach out.

3.Ok, so what exactly am I offering?

I may not fit into a traditional job title, but maybe that’s exactly why I could be useful.

What I’m offering is range. The kind David Epstein writes about in his book, and the kind I try to practice every day. I learn fast, adapt quickly, and move across contexts with ease.

That means I can:

  • Jump into a messy dataset and make it make sense: whether it’s ad hoc reporting, performance metrics, or just getting clarity on what’s going on.
  • Support cycles of work by organizing inputs before the cycle, clearing blockers during it, and summarizing takeaways after.
  • Clean up, update, or structure your documentation so people can actually find what they need. Or write down a process so it no longer lives only in someone’s head.
  • Step in for customer support when things get busy, or simply make sure every customer feels heard and cared for.
  • Help screen applications. Or help organize team meetups.
  • See where things get stuck, and make them flow again.

In short: when there’s something important to be done — something that isn’t code, but still really matters — I can be the person to go to.

4.And one last thing

I know this is an unusual way to introduce myself, but I figured if I’m going to reach out uninvited, the least I can do is make it interesting.

Below you can download my CV if you want to see more specifics of what I’ve done and the tools I know. But honestly, that’s not what matters most here.

What matters most is potential. The ability to grow into whatever the company needs. To keep things simple, useful, and moving.

I don’t have a technical background, and I’m not a developer — but for just about anything else, I can be your go-to person. I may not have all the answers right away, but I’m resourceful, thoughtful, and not afraid to figure things out—just like I did with this very webpage, built from scratch with zero technical background.

I know this isn’t a typical application. But if anything here resonates, I’d love to hear from you.

And if none of this leads anywhere? That’s okay too. I’m still glad I did it.

Thank you for reading!
